About

I am a lecturer at the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam (VU), where I mainly focus on teaching courses in statistics for various groups of students. I completed my PhD at the VU under supervision of Frank van der Meulen and Moritz Schauer. My research focusses on statistical inference for stochastic processes. Here, I focus on performing inference using simulation of bridges for a wide class of Markov process such as chemical reaction processes, solutions to SDEs and process on Riemannian manifolds.

Software

I frequently implemented software, mostly in Julia. Feel free to check out my GitHub account. Here is some software I have worked on.
  • CRMP.jl. Usef for forward simulation and statistical inference chemical reaction processes.

Junior Wiskunde Olympiade

As part of my work for the outreach committee of the mathematics department of the Vrije Universiteit, I organise the yearly final of the Junior Math Olympiad. Here we invite the 200 best participants of the Kangoeroe competition for classes 1 and 2 in high school to the VU to compete in the final.
